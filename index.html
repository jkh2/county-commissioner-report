1. Pre-Meeting Intelligence: Water Crisis & Agricultural Impact

Current Situation

Rio Grande River flows at 78% and Conejos River at 80% of normal (May 2025), with a 5% curtailment on groundwater wells due to a 400,000 acre-foot aquifer drop since 2002. Agriculture ($340M economic impact) is at risk, with only 1871 water rights served. Rio Grande Compact obligations strain resources.

Antonito-Specific Action: Collaborate with Conejos Clean Water to monitor Rio San Antonio water quality, ensuring no residual contamination from past waste transfers. Deadline: September 1, 2025.

Recent Developments

  • Rio Grande Flows: Only 1871 water rights served as of June 2025, an early curtailment.
  • Aquifer Conditions: 70% of 350,000 acre-foot recovery (2017) lost by 2018 due to pumping.
  • Water Export Proposal: Renewable Water Resources’ $600M pipeline opposed by six San Luis Valley counties.
  • Farmer Assistance: 13,000 acres in fallowing programs; sustainability challenges persist.

Recommended Actions

  • Vote to join San Luis Valley Planning Board by August 15, 2025, to oppose water exports.
  • Appoint liaison for USDA $500M drought recovery applications by August 7, 2025 (due September 15, 2025).
  • Advocate for state relief on $500/acre-foot groundwater fees at August 21, 2025, meeting.
  • Request state engineer briefing on Rio Grande Compact by August 21, 2025.

3. Constituent Request Tracking

Current Situation

Commissioners handle daily office hours (8-10 AM) with high volumes of constituent concerns, ranging from potholes to housing issues and neighbor disputes. No formal tracking system exists, risking oversight and inefficiency, especially with temporary courthouse facilities at 603 Main Street, Antonito, post-fire (July 2025).

Antonito Constituent Needs

  • Housing: 10+ weekly inquiries, particularly for affordable units (SLVRDG, 2025).
  • Infrastructure: Pothole complaints on County Road 15 affecting Antonito commuters.
  • Service Access: Courthouse fire (July 2025) relocated services to 603 Main Street, Antonito.

Recommended Action: Schedule weekly Commissioner office hours in Antonito (e.g., at S.P.M.D.T.U. Concilio Superior) starting August 15, 2025, to address resident concerns directly.

Recent Developments

  • Increased complaints about rural road conditions (e.g., County Road 15 potholes) reported July 2025.
  • Housing concerns rising, with 10+ weekly inquiries about affordable options for Hispanic communities.
  • Neighbor disputes (e.g., property boundaries) reported at 5-7 per week, requiring mediation.

Recommended Actions

  • Implement a digital constituent request tracking system by August 21, 2025, to log and prioritize issues.
  • Assign staff to categorize requests (e.g., infrastructure, housing, disputes) during office hours.
  • Schedule public response updates at August 21, 2025, meeting, per Colorado Sunshine Laws.

4. Regional Coordination Dashboard

Current Situation

Conejos County participates in the San Luis Valley County Commissioners Association (SLVCCA), Colorado Counties Inc. (CCI), and inter-county agreements (e.g., water, emergency services). Coordination is complex due to multiple boards and 66% federal land ownership requiring federal agency alignment.

Antonito Economic Snapshot

  • Tourism Revenue: $295,000 annually from Cumbres & Toltec Scenic Railroad and cannabis sales (2025).
  • Unemployment Rate: 4.2% (Conejos County, slightly above state average).
  • Median Household Income: $42,000 (2025, SLVRDG Statistical Profile).

Cannabis Revenue Strategies

Antonito’s $295,000 annual cannabis tax revenue, driven by dispensaries like Dispensary 7884 and High Valley Retail Cannabis, supports economic growth. Despite a 2022 sales downturn due to New Mexico’s legalization, strategic reinvestment can enhance infrastructure, tourism, and local businesses.

  • Infrastructure Investment: Allocate $100,000 of cannabis tax revenue to repair County Road 15 potholes, improving access for Antonito’s 647 residents and tourists. Deadline: September 15, 2025.
  • Small Business Grants: Launch a $50,000 grant program for Antonito entrepreneurs, prioritizing veteran-owned businesses like Dispensary 7884. Coordinate with SLVCCA by August 20, 2025.
  • Cannabis Tourism Campaign: Develop a “High Valley Experience” campaign, tying cannabis dispensaries to Cumbres & Toltec Scenic Railroad promotions, to attract visitors. Budget: $30,000, launch by September 1, 2025.
  • Regulatory Streamlining: Simplify local dispensary licensing to reduce regulatory burdens, addressing complaints about high excise taxes. Propose at August 21, 2025, county meeting.

Recommended Action: Present cannabis revenue strategies at the August 20, 2025, SLVCCA meeting to secure regional support and funding.
